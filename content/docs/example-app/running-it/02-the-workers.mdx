title: "02 — The workers"
description: "The per-service worker subcommands — projections, timers, inbox, outbox, and reservation work — and the order the cross-service scenario runs them."
---

The scenarios are driven by **bounded worker** invocations: each `…-worker` subcommand does one unit
of work and exits, so they compose into a deterministic sequence (and, in production, into
cron-style drains). This chapter is the operational reference for those subcommands. Read
[scaling the workers](/docs/keiro/explanation/scaling-the-workers) for the framework view of why
workers are separated from the write path.

## Incident Command worker

```text
incident-command-worker projections once
incident-command-worker projections rebuild
incident-command-worker timers once
incident-command-worker inbox consume-hospital-kafka once
incident-command-worker outbox enqueue-transfer-need <triage-id>
incident-command-worker outbox publish-dry-run once
incident-command-worker outbox publish-kafka once
```

- **projections once / rebuild** — drive the async projections (see
  [Incident Command · read models](/docs/example-app/incident-command/03-read-models-and-projections)).
- **timers once** — fire one due escalation timer
  ([escalation PM](/docs/example-app/incident-command/05-escalation-process-manager-and-timers)).
- **inbox consume-hospital-kafka once** — consume one hospital response and signal the evacuation
  workflow ([inbox](/docs/example-app/cross-service/03-inbox-and-consuming)).
- **outbox …** — enqueue a transfer need and publish the outbox
  ([outbox](/docs/example-app/cross-service/02-outbox-and-publishing)); `publish-dry-run` is the
  no-network variant used for deterministic span evidence.

## Hospital Capacity worker

```text
hospital-capacity-worker timers once
hospital-capacity-worker inbox consume-incident-kafka once
hospital-capacity-worker routing-region register <hospital-id> <region>
hospital-capacity-worker reservation-work setup
hospital-capacity-worker reservation-work enqueue-latest
hospital-capacity-worker reservation-work enqueue-malformed
hospital-capacity-worker reservation-work consume once
hospital-capacity-worker reservation-work confirm-latest
hospital-capacity-worker outbox enqueue-latest-reservation-response
hospital-capacity-worker outbox enqueue-latest-capacity-snapshot
hospital-capacity-worker outbox publish-dry-run once
hospital-capacity-worker outbox publish-kafka once
```

The **`reservation-work …`** subfamily is the operational surface of the
[pgmq queue](/docs/example-app/hospital-capacity/05-the-pgmq-reservation-work-queue): `setup` ensures
the queue and its DLQ exist; `enqueue-latest` turns the latest accepted transfer need into a job;
`consume once` drains one job (`runJobOnce`); `confirm-latest` signals the reservation workflow's
awakeable; `enqueue-malformed` is the deliberate DLQ probe.

## The order the cross-service scenario runs them

`run_mass_casualty_transfer` runs the workers in exactly this sequence (also the body of the root
`just run-workers` recipe):

```bash
incident-command-worker  outbox enqueue-transfer-need tri_01h455vb4pex5vsknk084sn02s
incident-command-worker  outbox publish-kafka once
hospital-capacity-worker inbox consume-incident-kafka once
hospital-capacity-worker routing-region register hosp_01h455vb4pex5vsknk084sn02s north
hospital-capacity-worker reservation-work setup
hospital-capacity-worker reservation-work enqueue-latest
hospital-capacity-worker reservation-work consume once
hospital-capacity-worker reservation-work confirm-latest
hospital-capacity-worker outbox enqueue-latest-reservation-response
hospital-capacity-worker outbox enqueue-latest-capacity-snapshot
hospital-capacity-worker outbox publish-kafka once
incident-command-worker  inbox consume-hospital-kafka once
```

Read top to bottom, that *is* the [end-to-end message flow](/docs/example-app/cross-service/00-the-message-flow):
publish the transfer need, consume it, route it to reservation work, drain and confirm the
reservation, publish the response, and consume it back to wake the evacuation workflow.
